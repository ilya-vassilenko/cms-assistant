2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The aim of this top-level policy is to define the purpose, direction, principles, and basic rules for information security management. This is to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data, resources, and people.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

This Policy is applied to the entire Frontify Information Security Management System (FISMS), as defined in the FISMS-002 FISMS Scope Document.

﻿

Users of this document are all employees of Frontify, as well as relevant external parties.

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

Information security is managed based on risk, business needs, and legal and regulatory requirements.

3 Chief Executives Statement of Commitment

Frontify is building the best and most user-friendly brand management platform – a platform that enables anyone to deliver compelling and consistent brand experiences. As a company, information processing is fundamental to our success, and safeguarding the security and privacy of this information is a top-level concern for us. We are committed to upholding our obligations to customers, privacy laws, ISO 27001, and other security and privacy frameworks, ensuring employee and customer data protection.

﻿

This document defines baseline security policies to meet legal, statutory, and regulatory requirements for using and protecting data held by Frontify. Furthermore, this Policy outlines expectations regarding the storage and processing of internal and external data to help ensure such data's confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy.

﻿

Data security and privacy are at the core of Frontify's company culture, and thus, every member of the company will be held accountable for helping to ensure a high level of protection for data held by Frontify.

﻿

Roger Dudler, CEO, February 20th, 2025

﻿

Peter Davida, CISO, February 20th, 2025

4 Information Security Terminology

Confidentiality – characteristic of the information by which it is available or disclosed only to authorized persons or systems.

﻿

Integrity – characteristic of the information by which it is altered or destroyed only by authorized persons or systems in an allowed way.

﻿

Availability – characteristic of the information by which it can be accessed and used by authorized persons when it is needed.

﻿

Information Security – preservation of confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

﻿

Information Security Management System – part of overall management processes that takes care of planning, implementing, maintaining, reviewing, and improving the information security.

﻿

Customer – customers are organizations or individuals that have established a formal business (e.g., NDA, Contract, Master Agreement, etc.) relationship with Frontify. A customer can, for example, be a company which aims to build a consistent brand and uses Frontify to do so.

﻿

Personal Information – personal information describes all data that contains or allows deducing with reasonable effort information about an individual person. Such data can contain but is not limited to information describing name, email address, Company, Picture, IP addresses.

﻿

Anonymized, aggregated information – data is considered being anonymized and aggregated if it is not possible to deduce with reasonable effort the identity and personal information of an individual person from that data.

﻿

Processing of personal data – personal data is being “processed” if such data:

  • is being obtained

  • is stored

  • is being modified

  • is being analyzed

  • is being provided to another party

  • is being published

  • is being destroyed

﻿

Lawful processing of personal data – personal data is considered to be processed in a lawful way if the data and respective analysis have been obtained from the respective person and/or processed in a way compliant with the national data privacy regulation.

﻿

Third Parties – a third party is defined as a person or legal entity that's not an employee of Frontify or of a company that has established formal business relations with Frontify, i.e., is not a customer of Frontify.

5 Frontify Information Security Management System

5.1 Mission

Our mission is to safeguard the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Frontify, its products, employees, and customers' assets.

﻿

﻿

5.2 Objectives

The objectives define the outcomes we seek to achieve the long-term mission.

﻿

  1. Strengthen the Information Security Office team and its capabilities to enable secure and innovative operations.
    ﻿

  2. Collaborate with internal and external stakeholders to promote resilience and reduce the occurrence and severity of security breaches.
    ﻿

  3. Build a security posture that’s best-in-class.

﻿

﻿

5.3 Initiatives

The initiatives define the "how" we seek to achieve the objectives and align with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.

﻿

  1. IDENTIFY, GOVERN & MANAGE: Effectively manage people, risk, resources, and governance to address security risks
    ﻿

  2. PROTECT: Establish a proactive posture against attacks
    ﻿

  3. DETECT: Strengthen systems to identify a security event
    ﻿

  4. RESPOND: Enhance measures to limit the impact of a potential security incident
    ﻿

  5. RECOVER: Build resilience to recover from a security incident
    ﻿

  6. DELIVER AND BE VISIBLE: Deliver operational excellence
    ﻿

  7. LEARN: Strive for continuous expansion of knowledge

﻿

5.5 Roles & Responsibilities

5.5.1 CISO

The CISO is responsible for reviewing the general Information Security mission, objectives, and initiatives and setting new ones if needed. A review must be done at least once a year.

﻿

Objectives for individual security controls or groups of controls are proposed by the Information Security Office team and approved by the CISO.

﻿

Frontify will assess the achievement of its objectives. The CISO is in charge of defining how these objectives will be measured. The CISO will analyze and evaluate the results of these measurements and report them frequently to the CEO. Additionally, during the annual management review, the CISO will share findings with the rest of the Senior Management team as necessary.

﻿

﻿

5.5.2 General

Information Security is everyone's responsibility to understand and adhere to the policies, follow processes, and report suspected or actual breaches. Specific roles and responsibilities for running the Frontify information security management system are defined and recorded in the policy FISMS-025 Roles and Responsibilities Policy﻿

﻿

The following role abbreviations are used in the present and all other FISMS documents:

  • CEO – Chief Executive Officer

  • CISO – Chief Information Security Officer

  • DPO – Data Protection Officer

﻿

﻿

5.4 Information Security Policy Framework

The present policy and the entire FISMS must be aligned with legal and regulatory requirements relevant to the organization in the field of information security, with a focus on data secrecy and personal data protection, as well as with contractual obligations.

﻿

A detailed list of all contractual and legal requirements is provided in the FISMS-007A List of Legal, Regulatory and Contractual Obligations.﻿

﻿

﻿

5.7 Training And Awareness

Policies are made readily and easily available to all employees and third-party users. A training and communication plan is in place to communicate the policies, processes, and concepts of information security. Training needs are identified, and relevant training requirements are captured in the document FISMS-023 Information Security and Awareness Training Policy.

﻿

﻿

5.8 Continual Improvement of the Management System

The Frontify Information Security Management System is continually improved. The FISMS-007 Continual Improvement Policy sets out Frontify’s approach to continual improvement, and a continual improvement process is in place.

6 Policy Compliance

6.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

6.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

6.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

6.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
