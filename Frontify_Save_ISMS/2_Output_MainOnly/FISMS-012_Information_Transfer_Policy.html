2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is ensuring the correct treatment when transferring information internally and externally to the company and to protect the transfer of information using all types of communication facilities.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

  • Frontify data, information, and systems

  • All Frontify employees

  • External third-party users (including but not limited to agencies, marketing service providers, etc.)

  • External third-party cloud services used by Frontify (e.g., SaaS tools)

  • Documented company information (e.g., presentations, wiki articles, Slack messages, emails, data in SaaS tools such as salary details, etc.)

  • Documented information determined as being necessary for the effectiveness of the Frontify Information Security Management System (FISMS)

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

  • Data transfer must comply with all legal and regulation legislation requirements including but not limited to the GDPR and Data Protection Act 2020.

  • Formal agreements that include non-disclosure and confidentially clauses must be in place for data sharing prior to the data transfer.

  • Personal data must not be transferred outside the European Economic Area without legal consent, justification, and legal mechanisms in place.

  • No personal or confidential information is to be transferred unencrypted.

  • All transfers are in line with the FISMS-011 Information Classification and Handling Policy

3 Information Transfer Policy

3.1 Virus Checking

Information that is transferred shall be virus checked before being sent or before being opened when received.

﻿

﻿

3.2 Information Encryption

Data and information that falls into the confidential and restricted category according to FISMS-011 Information Classification and Handling Policy, must either be encrypted before being transferred or must be sent through means that ensure encryption in transit.

﻿

Encryption credentials for username and password where used, must be shared via two separate and distinct communication methods. The preferred method is to share the username via email and the password via a voice call.

﻿

﻿

3.3 Preferred Transfer Method for Data and Information

3.3.1 Transfer Methods for Internal Data and Information

Frontify data and information sharing between employees may be exchanged through the following electronic communication channels:

  • Slack

  • Video & telephone calls

  • Google Drive

  • transfer of data via file-transfer protocols such as scp,

  • network filesystems such as NFS,

  • Email

  • download of files from the Internet

﻿

﻿

3.3.2 Transfer Methods for External Data and Information

Frontify data and information sharing with external parties may be exchanged through the following electronic communication channels:

  • Email

  • Slack (with NDA only)

  • External sharing drive on Google Drive

  • File-transfer protocols such as scp

  • Network filesystems such as NF

  • Frontify platform

﻿

﻿

3.4 Data Transfer by E-Mail

Email is never the best solution for transferring information as it is not secure and is not a guaranteed delivery mechanism. Thus, before sending Frontify data and information via email the information classification in FISMS-011 Information Classification and Handling Policy must be considered.

﻿

For confidential and restricted data and information, wherever possible and practicable, alternative secure methods for transfer must be considered.

﻿

Email communication should not be used to transfer unencrypted personal or confidential information.

﻿

Care must be taken as to what information is placed in the subject line of the email or in the accompanying message. Filename or subject line must not reveal the full contents of attachments or disclose any sensitive personal data.

﻿

Frontify company data and information must not be transferred via email to an employee’s private email account.

﻿

﻿

3.5 Data transfers by post/courier

Data transfers which occur via physical media such as paper reports, memory cards or CDs must only be dispatched via the company approved secure courier with a record of collection and a signature obtained upon delivery.

﻿

The recipient should be clearly stated on the parcel and the physical media must be securely packaged so that it does not break or crack.

﻿

The recipient should be advised in advance that the information is being sent so that they are aware when to expect the information.

﻿

The recipient must confirm safe receipt as soon as the information arrives. The sender responsible for sending the data is responsible for confirming the data has arrived safely.

﻿

Restricted data, such as personal information connected to a Frontify employee (e.g., insurance details, sickness leave notes, etc.) may only be opened by HR Operations.

﻿

﻿

3.6 Data transfers on removable media / memory sticks

As described in FISMS-021 Mobile Device Policy, the use of employee-owned removable media is prohibited. If there is a need (e.g., due to a customer demand) to use removeable media for the transfer of information, employees must inform the Information Security Office to gain approval.

﻿

Only company owned removable media is to be used for transferring information.

﻿

While transferring Frontify data or information, there must be clear instructions of the recipient’s responsibilities and instructions on what to do if they are not the intended recipient.

﻿

Issued removable media must be returned to the Information Security Office after completion of the transfer.

﻿

﻿

3.7 Telephones, Mobile Phones and General Conversations

As phone calls may be monitored, overheard, or intercepted (either deliberately or accidentally), employee must follow the listed precautions before sharing information with others:

  • Be conscious of your surroundings especially on public transport such as trains and public places such as coffee shops when discussing personal, confidential, or otherwise sensitive information.

  • Personal data must not be transferred or discussed over the telephone unless you have confirmed the identity and authorization of the recipient.

  • When using answer phones do not leave sensitive or confidential messages or include any personal data. Only provide a means of contact and wait for the recipient to speak to you personally.

  • When listening to answer phone messages left for yourself, ensure you do not play them in open plan areas which risks others overhearing. Delete them immediately after listening.

﻿

3.8 Social Media and Messaging Applications

All information transfers must be conducted through approved corporate channels, as outlined in section 3.3 Preferred Transfer Method for Data and Information.

﻿

The use of personal social media and consumer messaging applications (e.g., WhatsApp, Telegram, Threema) and social media platforms (e.g., Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn) for any business-related communication or information transfer is prohibited.

﻿

Exceptions apply only in the following cases:

  • Personal, non-business communication such as notifying a manager of illness, provided no company or client information is shared.

  • Communication of public information through company-managed social media accounts that have been explicitly approved by the Marketing team.

If the preferred transfer methods are not feasible in exceptional situations, the use of third-party messaging platforms for work-related communication must first be assessed and approved by the Information Security Office.

﻿

3.9 Lost or missing information

If it is discovered or suspected that information has been lost, is missing, did not arrive, or has been delivered to or overheard by the wrong recipient, employees are required to inform their team lead, the Information Security Office, or the Senior Management Team.

﻿

Lost or missing information must be treated as an incident and shall follow the principles in FISMS-017 Incident Response Policy.

4 Policy Compliance

4.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

4.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

4.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

4.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
