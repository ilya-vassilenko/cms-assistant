2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to set the rules for engaging and using cloud services. The processes and requirements outlined herein are designed to systematically evaluate and address potential risks associated with cloud service providers.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

  • All Frontify employees

  • External third-party users (including but not limited to agencies, marketing service providers, etc.)

  • All third-party cloud suppliers that process, store or transmit confidential or personal data

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

Third party cloud suppliers meet the requirements of the company, legislation, and regulation for data security. It is our policy that in line with Clause 5.3 Information Security for use of cloud services: ‘Cloud service agreements are often pre-defined and not open to negotiation.’ Our cloud providers fall into this category.

3 Cloud Service Policy

3.1 Cloud Service Supplier Management

Cloud service suppliers must be selected based on their ability to meet the needs of Frontify’s business goals as well as security, privacy and legal recommendations as identified and addressed during the cloud service supplier procurement process.

﻿

Cloud service suppliers may not be used without prior approval from the Information Security, Privacy and Legal Offices.

﻿

﻿

3.1.1 Cloud Service Supplier Risk Categorization

When engaging with a cloud service supplier, security, privacy and legal risks related to the respective service must be identified before from testing, purchasing, and/or using a cloud service supplier’s service.

﻿

A centralized approach must be in place to request, review and record cloud service supplier risks, contracts and any other relevant information needed as part of the ISO 27001:2022 standard.

﻿

As part of the risk categorization process, the following criteria must be provided by the future asset owner:

  • a use case description the cloud service supplier

  • the types of information processed & stored

  • CIA (confidentiality, integrity, availability) requirements

  • the amount of information processed & stored

  • the criticality of a supported business process

﻿

Depending on the use case, the CISO or the Information Security Office, shall have the right to request further clarification, if the provided details are not comprehensive enough to conclude the risk categorization process.

﻿

﻿

3.1.2 Cloud Service Supplier Risk Identification & Management

During the cloud service supplier selection process, the CISO or the Information Security Office must review the security measures of potential cloud service suppliers. This assessment is conducted in consideration of the criticality level assigned as part of the risk categorization process described in chapter 3.1.1.

﻿

The primary objective is to determine whether the security measures of the cloud service supplier align with the information security requirements mandated by Frontify. If discrepancies are identified, the CISO or the Information Security Office, the asset owner and/or the cloud service provider must evaluate whether there are suitable countermeasures that can be implemented to meet the required level of security assurance.

﻿

Should there be no alignment possible between Frontify’s information security requirements and a cloud service provider’s security offering, the CISO, or the Information Security Office, shall have the right to exclude a service provider from being tested, purchased, and/or used.

﻿

﻿

3.1.3 Third Party Supplier Register

All cloud services must be registered and recorded in the designated third party supplier register. As a minimum, the following key information must be captured within the register:

﻿

  • Cloud service name

  • A use case description

  • Risk Categorization (confidentiality, integrity, availability)

  • What type of data is being processed, stored or transmitted

  • Amount of to Frontify data processed

  • Cloud service owner

  • Cloud service main users (employees and/or departments)

  • A note on whether the cloud service provider is integrated with the Frontify app

  • List of other service providers that are integrated with the service provider in question

  • Relevant compliance certifications

  • The hosting location

  • Th date of the last security review

  • Any contractual documents that enable quick retrieval of key information such as, but not limited to total contract value, start, end & renewal dates, jurisdiction of the cloud service supplier

﻿

﻿

3.2 Cloud Service Information Security Requirements

Cloud service suppliers must be able to demonstrate adequate security controls and should hold relevant information security certifications that cover the services provided. As a minimum cloud service providers should have an ISO27001 certification or a SOC 2 Type 2 Certificate.

﻿

Should a cloud service provider not be able to meet the compliance certificate requirement listed above, the CISO or their designee shall assess the level of security based on the security considerations outlined in FISMS-015A Security Considerations & Clauses for Suppliers and Partners.

﻿

﻿

3.2.1 Cloud Service Supplier Contracts, and Data Processing Agreements

An appropriate contract, agreement and/or Data Processing Agreement must be in place and enforceable before engaging a cloud service supplier. As a minimum the following aspects must be addressed within the agreements:

  • Scope

  • Confidentiality

  • Processing of Personal Data

  • Security and Technical and Organizational Measures (TOMs)

  • Service Level Agreement (SLA)

  • Rights of Frontify

  • Intellectual Property Rights

  • Indemnification

  • Warranties

  • Liability

  • Insurance Requirements

  • Applicable Law and Place of Jurisdiction

  • Termination of the Agreement

﻿

A list of suggested clauses is given in FISMS-015A Security Considerations & Clauses for Suppliers and Partners.

﻿

﻿

3.2.2 Cloud Service Supplier Security Incident Management

Cloud service suppliers must have an appropriate security incident management process in place, ensuring that security incidents (accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Frontify data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities), are reported to the Information Security Office within 48 hours elapsed of becoming aware of the incident.

﻿

Wherever feasible, cloud service providers shall offer the option to:

  • Forward audit log events to Splunk

  • Generate log event with technical information and forward to splunk

  • Scan and detect malicious links, attachments, code (xss)

  • Review, delete, quarantine or release objects

  • Offer incident response capabilities

﻿

Should one of Frontify’s cloud service suppliers become subject to a security incident, the Frontify Information Security Office must manage such incidents as part of the incident management process outlined in FISMS-017 Incident Management Policy.

﻿

﻿

3.2.3 Cloud Service Supplier Contract Changes

Asset owners may propose contractual or use case changes or initiate a termination by informing the Information Security Office. If necessary, the CISO or the Information Security Office shall perform a new risk assessment before any changes are accepted.

﻿

﻿

3.2.4 Cloud Service Supplier End of Contract

At the end of the contract, where appropriate, practicable and relevant the asset owner must ask for a written statement that the cloud supplier has acknowledged the termination, and they met the contractual and legal obligations for the destruction of Frontify data processed, transmitted or stored. The confirmation must be recorder in the third party supplier registry for traceability purposes.

﻿

All access to Frontify data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities shall be revoked as outlined in FISMS-026 Access Control Policy.

﻿

﻿

3.3 Cloud Service Audit and Review

The CISO or the Information Security Office may perform security audits on cloud service suppliers directly or through channeling the asset owner’s duty. The extent of this audit and review is determined by the level of risk associated with the supplier.

4 Policy Compliance

4.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

4.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

4.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

4.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
