2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is the continual improvement of the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the Information Security Policy and Information Security Management System.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

  • All Frontify employees

  • External third-party users (including but not limited to agencies, marketing service providers, etc.)

  • The Frontify Information Security Management System (FISMS)

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

The Information Security Management System is continually improved and enhanced through addressing incidents and non-conformities with an effective corrective action and management process.

3 Continual Improvement Policy

To support the continual improvement of the Frontify Information Security Management System (FISMS), the CISO and/or the Senior Management must ensure the availability of means to ensure continuous monitoring of information security improvements, corrections, or non-conformities.

﻿

Sources for continual improvements may include but are not limited to the means described in this chapter.

﻿

﻿

3.1 Mandatory Sources for Continual Improvements

3.1.1 Internal Audits

The CISO or the Information Security Office, must plan and manage one internal audit per year to ensure cumulative coverage of the whole FISMS based on the requirements of the respective certification body.

﻿

The FISMS-008 Security & Audit Calendar must contain the following information about each individual internal audit:

  • Period of the audit (specifying dates, or month in which the audit is planned)

  • Scope of the audit (departments, processes, clauses of the standard, etc.)

  • Audit criteria (standards, legislation and regulations, internal documentation, corporate standards, and/or contractual obligations)

  • Auditing methods (review of documentation, interviews with employees, review of records, review of information systems, etc.)

  • By whom the audit is conducted (the number of auditors and specification of the audit team leader)

﻿

To conduct an internal audit, internal auditors must be selected in such a way that objectivity and impartiality are given. This shall counteract conflict of interest of the areas audited.

﻿

Criteria for appointing internal auditors conducting an ISO 27001:2022 audit:

  • Knowledge of the ISO/IEC 27001:2022 standard

  • Familiarity with management system auditing techniques

  • Knowledge of how information and communication technologies function to the extent that the person is familiar with the purpose of individual systems as well as the impacts on security processes and/or on business continuity

﻿

Employees and relevant stakeholders are required to provide the necessary cooperation and access to information during internal audits.

﻿

﻿

3.1.2 External Audits

The CISO or the Information Security Office must plan and manage one external audit per year to ensure cumulative coverage of the whole FISMS based on the requirements of the respective certification body.

﻿

External certification audits are conducted based on the certification body requirements.

﻿

External certification audits results are reported to and overseen by the Management Review Team.

﻿

External certification audits may result in a nonconformity requiring a corrective action or identifying an opportunity for improvement.

﻿

All employees and relevant stakeholders are required to provide the necessary cooperation and access to information during internal audits.

﻿

﻿

3.2 Ancillary Sources Continual Improvements

3.2.1 Client and third party audits

The basic principle is that access to company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities is forbidden unless expressly permitted to individual users or groups of users, thus access is granted on the principle of “least privilege”.

﻿

Access may only be handed out in line with an employee’s designation to perform assigned tasks and roles.

﻿

﻿

3.2.2 Incidents

Incidents must be managed based on the requirements of FISMS-017 Incident Management Policy. Learnings and outcomes of incidents must be managed in accordance with this policy.

﻿

﻿

3.2.3 Legal, Regulatory and Information Security Standards Change

Changes as a result of legal and regulatory requirements or changes to applicable standards for Information Security must be considered and managed cooperatively between the Information Security Office and Legal Office in accordance with this policy.

﻿

﻿

3.2.4 Change Management

Changes must be managed based on the requirements of FISMS-031 Change Management Policy. Learnings and outcomes based on the change management process must be managed in accordance with this policy.

﻿

﻿

3.2.5 Management Reviews

The yearly management review may introduce opportunities for improvement or require foundational changes to the Frontify Information Security Management System. Such changes must be managed in accordance with this policy.

﻿

﻿

3.2.6 Review of Company and/or Information Security Office Objectives

The review of company and/or Information Security objectives may introduce opportunities for improvement or require foundational changes to Frontify information security management system. Such changes must be managed in accordance with this policy.

﻿

﻿

3.2.7 Internal Exchanges (Meetings, 1on1s, etc.)

Internal or informal exchanges between Frontify employees may introduce opportunities for improvement to the Frontify Information Security Management System. Such changes must be managed in accordance with this policy.

﻿

﻿

3.3 Reporting Improvements, Corrections or Non-Conformities

Improvements, corrections or non-conformities may stem from direct involvement of the Information Security Office by being part of the sources listed in the previous chapter or may directly be communicated to the Information Security Office by Frontify employees through the following channels:

  • Direct message via Slack or Email to one of the Information Security Office team member

  • Ticket creation in the Security Slack Channel #security-and-it-questions

  • Email to security@frontify.com﻿

  • Employee voice service (anonymously)

﻿

﻿

3.4.2. Non-Conformities

A non-conformity is any failure to meet the requirements of the standards, internal documentation, regulations, contractual, and other obligations within the FISMS.

Non-conformities irrespective of their source, must immediately be addressed, planned, and managed by the CISO, the Information Security office or their appointed stakeholders based on the following cycle:

4 Policy Compliance

4.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

4.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

4.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

4.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
