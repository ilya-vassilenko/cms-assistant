2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to define rules for access to company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities, based on business and security requirements, to ensure the correct access to the correct information and resources by authorized employees and relevant third-parties.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

  • All Frontify employees

  • External third-party users (including but not limited to agencies, marketing service providers, etc.)

  • All systems and applications deemed in scope

  • Physical access is defined in the FISMS-035 Physical Security Policy﻿

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

Access control is granted on the principle of least privilege. Users are only provided access to the information they require to perform their tasks and role.

3 Access control policy

3.1 Principle of Access Levels

The basic principle is that access to company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities is forbidden unless expressly permitted to individual users or groups of users, thus access is granted on the principle of “least privilege”. 

﻿

Access may only be handed out in line with an employee’s designation to perform assigned tasks and roles. 

﻿

3.2 Role-Based Access

Wherever feasible, access to systems must be based on role. Access is granted by the business owner, system owner or data owner and formally approved.

﻿

3.3 Employee and Third-Party Access Preconditions

3.3.1 Confidentiality Agreements

Before accessing company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities, both employees and any other third party users, must have a signed non-disclosure agreement in place.

﻿

3.3.2 Unique Identifiers

  • Users must have a unique and identifiable username or identifier to ensure individual accountability, shared accounts are prohibited per default. 

  • Wherever passwords are required, the password policy in chapter 3.4 Password Complexity Requirements must be followed. 

  • Wherever feasible, users and accesses must be configured through Okta to ensure enforcement of the password policy.

  • Should there be a need for shared accounts due to technical or economic limitations the CISO, the Information Security Office, or their line manager must approve such shared accounts as a security exception.
    ﻿

3.4 Password Complexity Requirements

All passwords, no matter their purpose, must follow the below password policy:
﻿﻿

  • Passwords used for private purposes may not be used for business purposes.

  • Passwords must be protected by MFA on all systems.

  • Passwords may not be a single dictionary word, dialectal or jargon word from any language, or any of these words written backwards.

  • Passwords may not be based on personal data (e.g., date of birth, address, name of family member, etc.).

  • Passwords must be generated with 1Password (password manager) and must meet the following complexity requirements:

  • at least 12 characters long

  • at least 1 number

  • at least 1 special character

  • at least at least 1 upper- and lower-case letter

  • For systems where 1Password cannot be used (Okta, laptop, 1Password [password manager] itself), the password must meet the following complexity requirements: 

  • It is recommended to create a long sentence instead of an alphabetical/numeric password
    ﻿

3.5 Allocation of Access Rights and Privileges

  • Employees requiring access to company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities must follow the access request procedure to ensure traceability.

  • Asset owners or their designees are responsible to approve access to company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities based on the requirements as set out in this policy based on the least privilege principle.

  • Account creation, modification and deletion may only be done by the asset owner or their designee and must be fully documented. 

  • Asset owners must ensure that employees are positively identified and authenticated before gaining access to systems, services, or information. 

  • Asset owners or their designee must verify an employee’s identity in case password resets or changes are requested.
    ﻿

3.5.1 Privileged Accounts / Administrator Accounts

  • Administrator accounts may not be provided to employees without explicit need to perform an employee’s duty or role. This includes administrator access to company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities.

  • Wherever possible, administrator accounts must be set up in an addition to their normal account for the specific use on the completion of privilege and administrator tasks.

  • Privilege and administrator accounts may not be shared accounts, generic accounts and may not share the same password as other accounts. 

  • Privilege and administrator accounts must clearly be identifiable. 

  • Privilege and administrator accounts must be logged and monitored. 
    ﻿

3.6 Access Rights Review

User access to systems must be reviewed at least annually to ensure access are still appropriate and relevant. 

﻿

Inactive and dormant accounts (more than 90 days) should be investigated, and appropriate action must be taken by the asset owner, including the updating of required documentation. 
﻿

3.7 Employee Responsibilities

3.7.1 Password Handling

  • Passwords must adhere to the described password policy.

  • Passwords may not be shared with (but not limited to) other employees, external third-parties, senior management team members, system administrators or ITOps support staff.

  • Passwords may not be saved in an automated log-on systems (i.e. browsers) other than the approved means through the Information Security Office (i.e. Okta, 1Password).

  • Initial passwords provided to employees must be changed on first use.

  • Vendor supplied and default passwords must be changed immediately upon installation or first log in. 

  • Passwords that are suspected to have become public, must be changed immediately.

  • Passwords are to be kept confidential and not written down. 

  • Passwords may not be hard-coded or included in any scripts, code, or macros.

﻿

3.7.2 Sharing Restrictions

  • Sharing unique usernames and identifiers between employees is prohibited.

  • Users must safeguard their passwords and promptly report any suspected or actual compromise or unauthorized access to the Information Security Office.

  • Shared accounts must be configured through Okta or, if not possible, stored in the predefined 1Password team vaults. The means of configuration and sharing of shared accounts is determined by the CISO or the Information Security Office.

﻿

3.8 System Requirements

Wherever feasible, the following measures shall be considered before introducing new systems (i.e. cloud service providers):
﻿

  • Systems may not display a password when being entered.

  • Systems lock out must be set to 5 failed access attempts. 

  • System sessions that are idle for 7 days, require passwords to be entered to regain access. 

  • Systems should not provide help messages during the log-on procedure that would aid an unauthorized user.

  • Validate the log-on information only on completion of all input data. If an error condition arises, the system should not indicate which part of the data is correct or incorrect.

  • Systems must be secured against brute force log-on attempts.

  • Systems should log unsuccessful and successful attempts.

  • Systems should raise a security event if a potential attempted or successful breach of log-on controls is detected.

  • Systems may not transmit passwords in clear text over a network.

  • Systems must have industry-standard and up to date encryption standards.

﻿

3.9 Change of Contract, Role, or Termination

3.8.1 Frontify Employee Leavers

Upon termination of employment, People Operations must immediately inform the IT Operations and Information Security Office about the employee’s leave date to issue the access termination process. 

﻿

On the employee’s leave date, as a minimum, access to the main authentication system (Okta) must be revoked. Further accesses to company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities must be revoked fully 30 days after the employee’s leave date. Off-boardings must be recorded and retrievable in the main authentication system (Okta).

﻿

Usernames, passwords and any other authentication credentials of leavers must not be reused.

﻿

3.8.2 Third Party Leavers

Upon change or expiration of contractual relations with external third parties which have access to company data, information, systems, equipment, and facilities, the contract owner must immediately inform the IT Operations, Information Security Office and responsible asset owners to revoke or change access levels accordingly.

﻿

3.10 Monitoring and Reporting

Wherever possible, access to systems must be monitored and reported and actions that directly or indirectly affect or could affect the confidentiality, integrity or availability of data are managed via the Incident Management process.


﻿

4 Policy Compliance

4.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

4.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

4.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

4.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
