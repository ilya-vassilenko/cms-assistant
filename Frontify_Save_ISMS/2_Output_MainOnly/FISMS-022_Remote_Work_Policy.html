2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to manage the risks introduced by working remotely.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

  • All Frontify employees

  • External third-party users (including but not limited to agencies, marketing service providers, etc.)

  • All personal devices used to access, process or store company data or information

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

Remote sites and remote workers must adhere to the following policy to adequately protect company company data, information and systems.

3 Remote Work Policy

While Frontify owns physical office spaces (“Hubs”), working remotely (teleworking) at home or other remote places not categorized as a Hub or home, does not need to be explicitly authorized, except as otherwise advised by the CISO, the Information Security Office, or the line manager.

﻿

Team leaders must ensure that a deputy system is in place in case employees need to or have the wish to be absent for a longer time.

﻿

Teleworking does not include the use of mobile phones outside the organization's premises. The use of mobile phones is described in FISMS-021 Mobile Device Policy.

﻿

Regardless of the chosen place for working, employees must adhere to all other FISMS policies.

﻿

﻿

3.1 Working from Home

  • Employees may only use company-provided devices to conduct work, personal mobile devices are prohibited to be used when working from home except as indicated in chapter 4 in FISMS-021 Mobile Device Policy.

  • Employees must use secure and company-provided devices with up-to-date security software and other recommendations as outlined in FISMS-021 Mobile Device Policy and as otherwise advised by the Information Security Office or IT Operations Team.
    ﻿

  • Employees must ensure that company-provided devices are secured against unauthorized access to company data, information and, systems by family members, friends, acquaintances, etc.

  • Wi-Fi networks at home must be secured with strong passwords and encryption.

  • Wi-Fi networks must comply with minimum WPA2 or WPA3 standard.

  • Access to company data, information and, systems must follow the guidelines as outlined in FISMS-026 Access Control Policy.

  • If home printing is required, employees must follow the guidance of FISMS-011 Information Classification and Handling Policy which may include the requirement for a home shredder.

﻿

﻿

3.2 Working from Other Remote Locations

  • Employees intending to work from a country other than their official place of residence must submit a WorkFlex request and obtain prior HR approval before commencing remote work from that location.

  • Employees may only use company-provided devices to conduct work.

  • Personal mobile devices are prohibited to be used when working from other remote locations.

  • Employees are advised to not work with confidential or restricted data when working from other remote locations.

  • Wi-Fi networks at other remote locations must be secured with strong passwords and encryption.

  • Wi-Fi networks must comply with minimum WPA2 or WPA3 standard.

  • Unsecured Wi-Fi networks may not be used.

  • Employees may not use public, open networks when connecting to services.

  • It must be ensured that company-provided and personal mobile devices cannot be accessed by unauthorized persons (e.g., by glancing at a laptop screen or copying the data from a USB stick left unattended).

  • If applicable and possible, company-provided, and personal mobile devices shall be physically locked away.

  • Employees must always use the privacy shield (screen protector) when working in remote locations to prevent unauthorized access to company data, information, and systems through i.e. shoulder surfing.

  • When taking calls is necessary, employees must ensure they have access to private calling spaces (i.e. cubicles or meeting booths in a co-working space).

4 Reporting Incidents

Employees must immediately inform the Information Security Office in case of the following (but not limited) to scenarios:

  • unusual behavior of a company-provided device,

  • security incidents or breaches that stem from accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to company data, information and systems,

  • loss or theft of a company-provided device.

5 Policy Compliance

5.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

5.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

5.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

5.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
