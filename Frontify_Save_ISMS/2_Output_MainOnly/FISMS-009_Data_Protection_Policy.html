2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Data Protection Policy (hereinafter “Policy”) is to comply with Frontify’s legal and regulatory requirements under the applicable Data Protection Laws and the rights of data subjects.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

Users of this document are all employees of Frontify AG and its Affiliates (hereinafter “Frontify”), as well as relevant external parties.

﻿

The scope covers Personal Data as defined in this Policy and in accordance with the definition provided by Data Protection Laws.

﻿

  • Depending on the specific circumstances, in particular the location and entity processing the Personal Data, one or more Data Protection Laws might apply, inter alia the following:

  • Data Protection Act of Switzerland (hereinafter “DSG”)

  • General Data Protection Regulation of the European Union (hereinafter “GDPR”)

  • UK Data Protection Act 2018 and the UK General Data Protection Regulation (hereinafter “UK Privacy Laws”)

  • Certain other Data Protection Laws, which Frontify has to comply with, according to Agreements (e.g., DPA) with Customers such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (hereinafter “CCPA”) and the Singapore Personal Data Protection Act (hereinafter “PDPA”)

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

Data protection, including the Processing of Personal Data, is at the core of the company culture of Frontify and thus every employee of Frontify will be held accountable for helping to ensure the highest level of protection for Personal Data Processed by Frontify.

﻿

﻿

2.4 Data Protection Policy Statement

Based on the context of the Processing activities under applicable Data Protection Laws, Frontify is classed as:

﻿

  • Processor, in relation with our Customers Personal Data in accordance with Frontify’s DPA

  • and Controller, inter alia in relation to our employees and website visitors’ Personal Data and for certain processing activities of our Users in accordance with Frontify’s Privacy Notice (hereinafter “Privacy Notice”).

﻿

This Policy states our commitment to protect the privacy of Data Subjects such as Frontify’s Users, employees, and other individuals Frontify interacts with. Frontify has implemented the Frontify Information Security Management System which is aligned to the international standard ISO27001 to ensure that the Processing of Personal Data is conducted using best practice processes.

3 Legal Basis for Processing

For the processing of the Personal Data, Frontify relies on the lawful basis as stated in the last paragraph of each sub-section 5.1. - 5.19 of the Frontify Privacy Notice.

﻿

More details about the legal basis and further processing activities can be found in Frontify’s Record of Processing Activities and in the Privacy Notice.

4 Data Protection Principles

Frontify is committed to Processing Personal Data in accordance with its responsibilities under the Data Protection Laws and adheres to the following data protection principles as stated in Art. 5 GDPR.

﻿

﻿

4.1 Lawfulness, Fairness and Transparency

We have reviewed and documented the Personal Data we Process and determined the legal basis for processing. We inform the Data Subject about the collection, purpose and legal basis of the Processing of Personal Data in our Privacy Notice and carry out a privacy assessment for each new Processing activity in order to verify the lawfulness and fairness of the Processing.

﻿

﻿

4.2 Purpose Limitation

Frontify collects and Processes Personal Data exclusively for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes in accordance with Data Protection Laws and the Privacy Notice. In case we change the purpose of a specific Processing activity a new privacy assessment will be performed, and Data Subjects will be transparently informed.

﻿

﻿

4.3 Data Minimisation

Data minimization is defined as the collecting and retaining only the minimum amount of Personal Data which is relevant, adequate and necessary for a specific purpose.

﻿

We ensure that we collect only essential user information during account creation, such as username and email creation, which ensures that we Process only the minimum necessary Personal Data to deliver the core functionalities of our solutions and fulfilling our contractual obligations. We additionally conduct privacy assessments on a regular basis and we have a process in place that any new Processing activity needs to be assessed by the legal team in order to apply the data protection principles. At Frontify we have implemented several measures to ensure data minimization is applied such as anonymization, pseudonymization, limitation of data access (principle of least privilege), and data retention periods. Furthermore, the responsible individuals for a Processing activity (i.e. product managers) are instructed to only Process the Personal Data necessary for the intended purpose (Privacy by design) and the default settings need to be set so that the minimal amount of Personal Data is Processed (Privacy by default).

﻿

﻿

4.4 Accuracy

Data accuracy refers to the principle of keeping Personal Data accurate and up to date. This also means that every reasonable step must be taken to ensure that Personal Data that are inaccurate, having regard to the purposes for which they are processed, are erased, or rectified without delay.

﻿

In the context of the Frontify Services provided to our Customers, the Customer shall have the sole responsibility for the accuracy, quality, and legality of Customer Personal Data, while Frontify shall offer the required functionalities to perform such controls by the means of the Personal Settings functionality on the Frontify Platform.

﻿

Our employees have access to their Personal Data Processed in our systems and can update it by themselves at any time on our HR management tool provided by Personio.

﻿

﻿

4.5 Storage Period Limitation

Data storage period limitation refers to the practice of defining and adhering to a specific timeframe for retaining stored data, in compliance with legal, regulatory, or organizational requirements, and to minimize privacy and security risks associated with prolonged data retention.

﻿

In relation to the Frontify Services, the Processing activities are performed during the contract term as agreed in the Agreement with the Customer, plus the period from expiry of such term until deletion of all Customer Personal Data by Frontify. All Customer Personal Data is deleted within 90 days after the termination of the Agreement except for Personal Data archived in back-ups (which are deleted after 365 days) and Personal Data in records for legal retention obligations. This all in accordance with the provisions of our DPA and the Agreement.

﻿

To abide to such period limitation(s), data must be processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security, including protection against unauthorised or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage, using appropriate technical or organisational measures.

﻿

Frontify has implemented the Frontify Information Security Management System (the FISMS) in line with ISO 27001 the International Standard for Information Security which highlights our security and data protection efforts and for which we are audited on a yearly basis. The FISMS also apply to Personal Data stored in back-ups and in records for legal retention obligations.

5 Personal Information Classification and Handling

Personal Data classification and handling is in line with the FISMS-011 Information Classification and Handling Policy.

6 Personal Information Retention

Personal Data is retained and destroyed in line with the FISMS-011 Information Classification and Handling Policy and the FISMS-10 Data Retention Policy.

7 Personal Information Transfer / Transmit

Personal Data is transferred in line with the FISMS-012 Information Transfer Policy and employees ensure the appropriate level of security in line with the policy and company processes.

8 Personal Information Storage

Personal Data storage is in line with the FISMS-011 Information Classification and Handling Policy, FISMS-035 Physical Security Policy, FISMS-028 Cryptographic Control Policy, FISMS-027 Backup Policy, and the FISMS-010 Data Retention Policy.

9 Breach

Frontify employees, suppliers, vendors as well as any other business partner of Frontify who are in contact with information and/or systems of Frontify must report any breach or event to incidents@frontify.com or directly to the CISO. Depending on the incident, stakeholders can also report any privacy related query, breach or other relevant information to the Privacy Team at privacy@frontify.com.
﻿

Additionally it is also possible to submit a privacy issue according to the process described in the Privacy Notice.

﻿

Data breaches are treated in line with the FISMS-017 Incident Management Policy.

10 The Rights of Data Subjects

If Data Subjects wish to exercise any of its rights (i.e. Data Subject Request) or for any questions about the Privacy Notice or, in general, relating to the Processing of the Personal Data at Frontify, they can do so by writing an email to privacy@frontify.com.

﻿

10.1 The right to be informed

Individuals have the right to be informed about how we use their Personal Data.

This includes:

  • The name and contact details of our organisation.

  • The name and contact details of our representative (if applicable).

  • The contact details of our data protection officer (if applicable).

  • The purposes of the processing.

  • The lawful basis for the processing.

We provide such information in the Privacy Notice and when we act as a Processor in the DPA.

﻿

﻿

10.2 The right to access

  • Individuals have the right to access their personal data.

  • This is commonly referred to as subject access.

  • Individuals can make a subject access request verbally or in writing.

  • We have one month to respond to a request.

  • We cannot charge a fee to deal with a request in most circumstances.

Data Subjects (Frontify Users) can at any time modify their Personal Data and access it in the personal settings on the Frontify Platform.

﻿

﻿

10.3 The right to rectification

  • The GDPR includes a right for individuals to have inaccurate personal data rectified or completed if it is incomplete.

  • An individual can make a request for rectification verbally or in writing.

  • We have one calendar month to respond to a request.

  • In certain circumstances we can refuse a request for rectification.

Data Subjects (Frontify Users) can at any time modify their Personal Data and access it in the personal settings on the Frontify Platform.

﻿

﻿

10.4 The right to erasure (the right to be forgotten)

  • The GDPR introduces a right for individuals to have personal data erased.

  • The right to erasure is also known as ‘the right to be forgotten’.

  • Individuals can make a request for erasure verbally or in writing.

  • We have one month to respond to a request.

  • The right is not absolute and only applies in certain circumstances.

  • This right is not the only way in which the GDPR places an obligation on us to consider whether to delete personal data.

Data Subjects (Frontify Users) can at any time modify their Personal Data and access it in the personal settings on the Frontify Platform.

﻿

﻿

10.5 The right to restrict processing

  • Individuals have the right to request the restriction or suppression of their personal data.

  • This is not an absolute right and only applies in certain circumstances.

  • When processing is restricted, we are permitted to store the personal data, but not use it.

  • An individual can make a request for restriction verbally or in writing.

  • We have one calendar month to respond to a request.

If a Frontify user does not interact with the Frontify Platform, we do not actively Process any Personal Data of the User. The Data Subject can also unsubscribe and modify their notification settings using the link in the footer implemented in every email communication of Frontify.

﻿

﻿

10.6 The right to data portability

  • The right to data portability allows individuals to obtain and reuse their personal data for their own purposes across different services.

  • It allows them to move, copy or transfer personal data easily from one IT environment to another in a safe and secure way, without affecting its usability.

  • Doing this enables individuals to take advantage of applications and services that can use this data to find them a better deal or help them understand their spending habits.

  • The right only applies to information an individual has provided to a controller.

The Customer can request to have an export of their data (incl. Personal Data) in accordance with art. 13.3 of the GTC and art. 5.3 para. 2 of the DPA.

﻿

﻿

10.7 The right to object

  • The GDPR gives individuals the right to object to the processing of their personal data in certain circumstances.

  • Individuals have an absolute right to stop their data being used for direct marketing.

  • In other cases where the right to object applies, we may be able to continue processing if we can show that we have a compelling reason for doing so.

  • We must tell individuals about their right to object.

  • An individual can make an objection verbally or in writing.

The Data Subject can also unsubscribe and modify their notification settings using the link in the footer implemented in every email communication of Frontify.

﻿

﻿

10.8 Rights in relation to automated decision making and profiling

Individuals have the right not to be subject to a decision when:

  • It is based on automated processing, and

  • It produces a legal effect or a similarly significant effect on them.

At Frontify we do not have automated decision making or profiling processes in place.

11 Definitions

To ensure the company understands its obligations to the protection of Personal Data, the following definitions apply and are based on current understanding of these terms in Data Protection Laws as well as in line with the DPA and Privacy Notice of Frontify.

﻿

﻿

11.1 Affiliate

“Affiliate” means any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with the subject entity. “Control”, for purposes of this definition, means direct or indirect ownership or control of more than 50% of the voting interests of the subject entity, or otherwise having the power to govern the financial and the operating policies or to appoint the management of the subject entity.

﻿

﻿

11.2 Agreement

“Agreement” means the Frontify License Agreement, Order form, or other written or electronic agreement concluded between Frontify and Customer for the use of the Frontify Services, including all its attachments, in particular, but not limited to, the Offer, the GTC, the Service Level Agreement and any other additional document governing the contractual relationship between Frontify and Customer.

﻿

﻿

11.3 Controller

“Controller” means the entity which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data.

﻿

﻿

11.4 Customer

“Customer” means the contractual party under the Agreement with Frontify, including such customer’s Affiliates.

﻿

﻿

11.5 Customer Data

“Customer Data” means data, including Customer Personal Data, submitted, stored, sent, or received via the Frontify Services by Customer, its Affiliates, or Users.

﻿

﻿

11.6 Data Protection Laws

“Data Protection Laws” means all laws and regulations applicable to the Processing of Personal Data under the Agreement, which may include but is not limited to the GDPR, the laws of the EEA and its member states, Switzerland, the United States of America, and the United Kingdom.

﻿

﻿

11.7 Data Subject

“Data Subject” means the individual to whom Personal Data relates.

﻿

﻿

11.8 Data Subject Request

“Data Subject Request” is a demand made by a Data Subject that seeks to exercise the Data Subject’s right to access, rectify, erase, transfer, or port Personal Data or to restrict the Processing of Personal Data in accordance with Chapter III GDPR.

﻿

﻿

11.9 Frontify Platform

“Frontify Platform” means the software supplied by Frontify to Customer for use via the Internet, namely the all-in-one web-based brand management SaaS solution, the mobile app, and the desktop app offered by Frontify.

﻿

﻿

11.10 Frontify Services

“Frontify Services” means the services offered by Frontify and purchased by Customer under the Agreement, both currently and in the future, including the subscription to the Frontify Platform.

﻿

﻿

11.11 Personal Data

“Personal Data” means any information that directly or indirectly identifies a Data Subject under the Data Protection Laws.

﻿

11.12 Processor

“Processor” means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body which Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

﻿

﻿

11.13 Processing

“Processing” or “Process” means any operation or set of operations which is performed upon Customer Personal Data, whether or not by automatic means, such as collection, recording, organization, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, blocking, erasure or destruction.

﻿

﻿

11.14 User

“User” means any natural person who is authorized to use the Frontify Services under the Agreement.

12 Policy Compliance

12.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

12.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the Legal Team and the CISO.

﻿

﻿

12.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

12.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
