2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to ensure the protection of Frontify's intellectual property rights and the license rights of Frontify for the use of intellectual property rights of third parties.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

  • All Frontify employees

  • All Frontify external service providers

  • External third-party license users (including but not limited to customers, agencies, marketing service providers, etc.)

  • External third-party licensors

  • Frontify's affiliates

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principles

Frontify has the ownership of or has obtained all the rights to access, use, and benefit from all intellectual property rights in connection with its business activities. Frontify's use of proprietary products and services of third parties are in full compliance with legal, statutory, regulatory, and contractual requirements.

3 Frontify Intellectual Property Rights

Frontify shall protect its intellectual property rights on three dimensions: 1) IP developed by employees in the course of their employment; 2) IP developed by external suppliers, including their sub-contractors; 3) IP of Frontify licensed to third-party users.

﻿

﻿

3.1 Employment contracts

Frontify employs workforce in different jurisdictions which shall adhere to the clauses listed in FISMS-007A List of Legal, Regulatory and Contractual Obligations.

﻿

All Frontify employment contracts shall contain specific clauses aimed at assigning automatically, or by producing separate specific documentation, IP rights developed by employees in the course of the employment relationship with Frontify in accordance with relevant local laws (e.g. Swiss Code of Obligations; US Copyright Act of 1996; UK Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988; French Intellectual Property Code; and any other applicable legislation).

﻿

To the extent an automatic assignment of IP rights is not possible or if such assignment requires the production of additional documentation to be formalized, the employees are obliged to reasonably cooperate with Frontify to 1) obtain, perfect, and assign the relevant IP rights and 2) maintain, protect, and enforce them against third-party claims.

﻿

Frontify also ensures protection of such IP rights at group level, For that, Frontify has signed transfer pricing agreements for the legal provision of services to Frontify in order to implement a cost+ model. In that regard, Frontify has included the obligation of the affiliate to cooperate with and, if needed by applicable law, execute any additional documents reasonably requested by Frontify to evidence all such assignments of intellectual property.

﻿

3.2 External Suppliers

Frontify shall engage external suppliers and contractors either through means such as:

  • professional employment organizations

  • or by signing separate agreement (e.g. Freelancer Agreement; Service Agreements; Partnership Agreement, etc)

External contractors shall be subject to FISMS-015 Third Party Service Provider Policy and the clauses defined in FISMS-007A List of Legal, Regulatory and Contractual Obligations.

﻿

For contractors outsourced by professional employment organizations Frontify shall have layers of protection such as:

  • 1) clear IP obligations between Frontify and the professional employment organizations, obliging the later to ensure the transfer of all IP rights created by the contractors to Frontify;

  • and 2) either the inclusion of standard IP transfer clauses in the employment contracts of the contractors with the professional employment organizations for the benefit of Frontify, or separate IP transfer agreements between the contractors and Frontify.

﻿

For contracts with external suppliers that provide specific services to Frontify on a project specific case (freelancers, agencies, etc.), the contracts shall include specific IP clauses transferring the IP rights, including IP rights developed by such suppliers' sub-contractors, to Frontify and shall adhere to FISMS-015 Third Party Service Provider Policy.

﻿

3.3 Third party license users

In the course of its business, Frontify licenses its IP rights to third party users (customers, partners, etc.). All relevant license rights and restrictions shall be included in the contract between the parties.

﻿

Such agreements as defined in FISMS-007A List of Legal, Regulatory and Contractual Obligations shall include clauses about the right of use of IP rights of each party, including relevant restrictions and prohibitions, such as the prohibition to copy, modify, translate, or create derivative works based on the IP rights of Frontify, use any IP right without the express consent of Frontify and use, register, or attack any trademark of Frontify.

4 Intellectual Property Rights licensed to Frontify

In the course of its business, Frontify has obtained the license to use and/or commercialize third-party IP in different domains: 1) software provided by third parties through software licensing; 2) open-source components embedded in the software provided by Frontify; 3) IP rights of Frontify software users (customers, partners, etc.).

﻿

﻿

4.1 Software Licensing

4.1.1 Software Licensing

When procuring software products and services from third parties Frontify shall adhere to the following:

  • Software used by Frontify shall be acquired through official channels and where a purchase is required to use the software evidence of a valid license is retained.

  • Software shall be purchased in accordance with the procurement process under FISMS-014 Cloud Service Provider Policy﻿

  • Software contract shall be reviewed and negotiated in accordance with FISMS-015A Security Considerations & Clauses for Cloud Service Providers.

  • Software shall be used in line with the licencing agreement.

  • A software license register shall be maintained.

  • Software license reviews shall be conducted at least annually or after significant change.

  • Software patching levels shall be maintained in line with manufacturer recommendations.

  • Only software that is supported by the manufacturer is to be used.

  • Software shall only installed by authorised, assigned persons and in alignment with FISMS-021 Mobile Device Policy.

﻿

4.1.2 Software License Assets Register

All software shall be registered and recorded in the Software License Assets Register. The following shall be captured as a minimum, wherever feasible:

  • Software Name

  • Person Responsible

  • Whether the software is free or paid

  • Number of licenses purchased

  • Number of licenses in use

  • Where the software is hosted

  • The last review dates

  • The next review dates

  • Duration of the license

  • Who conducted the review

  • Agreements in place

﻿

4.2 Open-Source Software

4.2.1 Open-source software

Frontify leverages from open-source components. Frontify shall adhere to the following:

  • Software is purchased in accordance with the process under FISMS-014 Cloud Service Provider Policy.

﻿

4.2.2 Open-Source Software License Assets Register

All open source software embedded in the Frontify software shall be registered and recorded in the FISMS-005A Inventory of Assets.

The following shall be captured as a minimum wherever feasible:

  • Open-Source Software Name

  • Version

  • License Type

  • Hompage

  • Sourcecode-Reposiitory

  • Usage in the Frontify software

  • PR Author

  • PR Approvers

  • PR Approval Date

  • PR Link

﻿

﻿

4.3 IP rights of Frontify software users

The core business of the SaaS solution licensed by Frontify is that of asset management and asset transformation which means that customers use the platform to upload and transform images, logos, icons, fonts, documents, and other kinds of customer's data into the platform.

﻿

For this reason, in the course of providing the Frontify software, Frontify processes data and information (customer data) that is considered third party IP rights.

  • Customer data shall clearly be defined in the Frontify's General Terms and Conditions for Enterprise Customers as detailed in FISMS-007A List of Legal, Regulatory and Contractual Obligations.

  • Customer data also includes feedback from users. The contractual arrangements between Frontify and its customers shall clearly lay down the legal ground on which Frontify is entitled to use IP rights of customers on a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free, non-transferable, irrevocable basis.

  • Frontify shall have the right to use customer data to (i) delivering, monitoring, enhancing, and improving the services provided by Frontify in accordance with the content of the agreement or where required or authorized by law, and (ii) delivering and providing customer support services, and related technical support.

﻿

4.4 Risk Management

Software/Open Source shall be assessed against the risk to the organisation to information security before acquisition and usage as defined in FISMS-014 Cloud Service Provider Policy.

﻿

﻿

4.5 Cloud Service Supplier Selection

Software selected shall be based on its ability to meet the needs of the business as defined in FISMS-014 Cloud Service Provider Policy.

﻿

﻿

4.6 Changes to Software/opensource

Changes to the Software used shall, wherever feasible, follow the Change Management Policy defined in FISMS-031 Change Management Policy.

﻿

5 Policy Compliance

5.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

5.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

5.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

5.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
