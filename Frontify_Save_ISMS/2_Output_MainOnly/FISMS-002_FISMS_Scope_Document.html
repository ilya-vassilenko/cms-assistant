2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this document is to clearly define the boundaries of the Information Security Management System of Frontify (FISMS). In particular, the scope focuses on the assets, essential for the company's business operations.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

This document is applied to all documentation and activities within the FISMS.

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

Have a defined scope in place for ISO 27001:2022.

3 The FISMS Scope

The organization needs to define the boundaries of the Information Security Management System in order to decide which information it wants to protect. Such information will need to be protected no matter if it is additionally stored, processed, or transferred in or out of the FISMS scope. The fact that some information is available outside of the scope doesn't mean the security measures won't apply to it – this only means that the responsibility for applying the security measures will be transferred to a third-party who manages that information.


﻿

Taking into account the legal, regulatory, contractual, and other requirements, the FISMS scope is defined as specified in the following items:

﻿

﻿

3.1 Scope Statement

The scope of the ISMS encompasses all assets, operational processes, and services associated with the Frontify application and the entirety of the company's business operations, including all teams and business units, regardless of their physical location or execution method.

﻿

﻿

3.2 Processes and Services

The scope of FISMS covers all processes and services which are connected with the Frontify assets, essential for the company's business operations, regardless of where the process or service is carried out:

  • Development and maintenance of the Frontify Application

  • Operation and maintenance of the server infrastructure

  • Operation and maintenance of the local infrastructure

  • Selection, training, and supervision of employees

  • Legal services like compliance with laws and GDPR

  • Selection, supervision, and cooperation of third parties and subcontractors

﻿

3.3 Information and Data

Information and data are all electronically stored, transmitted, and processed information which are connected with the Frontify assets, essential for the company's business operations, irrespective of where the process or service is carried out.


﻿

3.3.1 In Scope

  • Customer data

  • Employee data

  • Financial data

  • Business secrets

  • Paper documents

﻿

﻿

3.4 Organizational Units

The scope of FISMS covers all organizational units and employees which are connected with the Frontify assets, essential for the company's business operations, irrespective of where the process or service is carried out.


﻿

3.4.1 In Scope

All internal Teams:

  • Sales and all its sub-teams

  • Technology and all its sub-teams

  • Marketing and all its sub-teams

  • Finance & Legal

  • Technical Operations

  • People and all its sub-teams

  • Customer Success and all its sub-teams

  • Information Security & IT Operations Team

  • Operations and all its sub-teams

  • Partnerships

  • Product

﻿

﻿

3.4.2 Out of Scope

  • Currently all teams are part of the FISMS.

3.6 Frontify & Its Context

3.6.1 Understanding the Context of the Organization

As per the recommendations of point 4.1 of ISO/IEC 27001:2022, Frontify has worked out internal and external issues, which have a direct or indirect impact on the established FISMS.

﻿

﻿

3.6.2 External Issues

3.6.2.1 Technical Issues of External Origin

Technical issues of external origin which are connected with the software or cloud services used by Frontify may have an impact on the services provided to customers and on the established FISMS.
﻿

The mitigation of such risks takes place in FISMS-013 Risk Management Policy.


﻿

3.6.2.2 Natural Disasters

Natural disasters may have an impact on the availability of the service provided to the customers and therefore impacts the FISMS also.
﻿

The mitigation and management of such risks takes place in FISMS-018 Business Continuity Policy.


﻿

3.6.2.3 Non-Compliance with Applicable Laws & Regulations

Compliance with applicable laws and regulations is ensured in all countries where Frontify:
﻿

  • has a headquarter

  • has a subsidiary

  • has an operational business (contracts with data providers, data customers, vendors, partners, and others)

Non-compliance with applicable laws and regulations poses a great risk to the FISMS. Applicable laws for Frontify must be listed in FISMS-007A List of Legal, Regulatory and Contractual Obligations﻿

﻿

Compliance with other laws and regulations that affect contracts (employee, data customer, data provider, and other types) is ensured through a legal review done by the Legal Team.


﻿

3.6.3 Internal Issues

3.6.3.1 Technical Issues of Internal Origin

Technical Issues of Internal Origin are addressed in FISMS-013 Risk Management Policy and mitigated as part of the usual bug process, as an OKR for the next quarter, or immediately for urgent issues.


﻿

3.6.3.2 Changes in Employment Relationship

Changes in employment relationship and other issues connected with employees are addressed in FISMS-013 Risk Management Policy.

﻿

﻿

3.7 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties

Based on recommendations of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 regarding point 4.2 “Understanding the needs and expectations of interested parties" of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Frontify has identified the following parties and their needs and expectations which are relevant to the FISMS. The list of interested external parties is not conclusive, meaning that it can change depending on the company or market situation. Additionally, the listed external parties have been chosen due to the fact that they have the most impact on Frontify as a company and the respective FISMS.

﻿

﻿

4 Roles & Responsibilities

Roles and responsibilities are described in detail in FISMS-025 Roles and Responsibilities.

5 Policy Compliance

5.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

5.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

5.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

5.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
