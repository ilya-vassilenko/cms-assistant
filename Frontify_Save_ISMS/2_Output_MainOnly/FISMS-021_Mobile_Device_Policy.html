2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to manage the risks introduced by using company-provided and personal mobile devices and to secure company data, information and systems.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

  • All Frontify employees

  • External third-party users (including but not limited to agencies, marketing service providers, etc.)

  • All company-provided devices used to access, process or store company data, information and systems

  • All personal devices used to access, process or store company data, information and systems

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principles

Mobile devices (laptops, phones, iPads, etc.) must have adequate protection.

3 Mobile Device Policy

This policy is split into two section covering policies for company-provided and employee-owned mobile devices.

﻿

﻿

3.1 Company-Provided Mobile Device Policy

The policies detailed in this chapter, apply to company-provided mobile devices, both when it is used for professional (i.e. reading emails) and for private purposes (i.e. when watching Netflix), within and outside of Frontify’s Hubs.

﻿

﻿

3.1.1 Ownership of Company Data, Information, and Systems

Frontify company data, information and systems which are accessed, stored, transferred, or processed by a company-provided mobile device always remains under the ownership of Frontify.

﻿

If necessary, due to non-compliance with this policy or because of a change in an employee’s working relationship, Frontify has the right to view, edit, and delete all Frontify data, information, and systems on the company-provided mobile device.

﻿

﻿

3.1.2 Mobile Device Registration

  • Devices must automatically be recorded in the FISMS-005A Inventory of Assets by the Information Security Office.

  • Devices must automatically be assigned to a named individual and may not be shared by the Information Security Office.

  • Devices must automatically have a Jamf profile installed at all times by the Information Security Office.

  • Devices must automatically have an Okta profile installed at all times which is maintained by the Information Security Office.

﻿

﻿

3.1.3 Mobile Device Security

  • Devices must run on up-to-date operating systems.

  • Devices must have appropriate encryption, anti-virus and access control systems installed where necessary and available.

  • Devices must not be rooted or backdoored.

  • Devices must be manageable remotely where necessary.

  • Devices must have a firewall active at all times.

  • Devices must be enabled to have their contents remotely wiped in the event of loss or theft wherever feasible. This feature is enabled prior to the user being given access to the mobile device and mobile devices have their automatic lockout enabled.

  • Where remote deletion or management is not possible, the IT Operations team has the right to physically change or manage devices.

﻿

﻿

3.1.4 Mobile Back Up

Mobile devices (incl. locally stored filed on a desktop or local folder) are not backed up by default, thus safeguarding and backing up Frontify data, information and systems is the responsibility of the employee.

﻿

Employees must only use Google Drive or the SaaS tool in question to perform work as a means of backup. The use of other backup mechanisms is prohibited, except the Information Security Office has issued an exception.

﻿

﻿

3.1.5 Employee Responsibilities

  • Employees are personally responsible for the device.

  • Employees must ensure operating system and application patching is up to date.

  • Employees must ensure encryption and antivirus where installed is enabled.

  • Employees must ensure the device is not left unattended and when not in use or physically secured.

  • Employees may only access company information, data, and systems required for role in line with FISMS-026 Access Control Policy.

  • Employees must ensure that company-provided devices are secured against unauthorized access to company data, information and, systems by family members, friends, acquaintances, etc.

  • Employees must return the company-provided mobile device, unless otherwise agreed with the Information Security Office, when it no longer required.

﻿

﻿

3.1.6 Prohibited Usage of Company-Provided Devices

Employees may not perform the following actions on company-provided mobile devices:

  • download, install, or distribute harmful software from the internet without prior approval of the Information Security Office

  • install or use unlicensed software and services, where licensing would apply

  • install or use software and services that are listed in the Appendix 1: Prohibited Software﻿

  • browse websites that may pose security risks, such as those associated with malware, phishing, or hold illegal content

  • engage in any activities that violate the organization's acceptable use policy

  • change a company-provided mobile device’s settings

  • store personal (internal and customer), confidential, and restricted data (see FISMS-011 Information Classification and Handling Policy)

  • allow unsupervised access to anyone else except the employee who is the owner of the company-provided mobile device

  • store illegal materials on the company-provided mobile device

  • transfer company data, information and, systems to other devices or between corporate and personal accounts (Android) that do not comply with this policy

﻿

﻿

3.1.7 Taking Company-Provided Mobile Devices Offsite

When taking company-provided mobile devices offsite, the policies described in FISMS-022 Remote Work Policy must be followed.

﻿

﻿

3.1.8 Return of Company-Provided Mobile Devices upon Termination of Contract

Upon termination of an employment contract or other contracts, employees must return all data, information, and systems as well as company-provided mobile devices to Frontify.

﻿

IT Operations may copy and/or remove all files related to Frontify.

4 Bring Your Own Device Policy (BYOD)

The policies detailed in this chapter, apply to employee-owned devices, both when it is used for professional (i.e. reading emails) and for private purposes (i.e. when watching Netflix), within and outside of the organization's premises.

﻿

Employee-owned devices (or devices owned by employee’s family, friends, acquaintances etc.) may not be used to access, process or store company data, information, and systems, with the exception of:

  • Employee-owned smartphones

  • Employee-owned tablets

  • Employee-owned wearable devices (i.e. smart watches, fitness trackers, etc.)

﻿

Any other employee-owned device, that is not listed, is prohibited to be used for professional purposes. By means of example, the following (but not limited to) employee-owned mobile devices are prohibited; memory cards, digital cameras plugged into company-provided mobile devices, personal USB sticks etc.

﻿

﻿

4.1 Ownership of Company Data, Information, and Systems

Frontify company data, information and systems which are accessed, stored, transferred, or processed by an employee-owned mobile device always remains under the ownership of Frontify.

﻿

If necessary, due to non-compliance with this policy or because of a change in an employee’s working relationship, Frontify has the right to view, edit, and delete all Frontify data, information, and systems on the employee-owned mobile device in the presence of the device owner.

﻿

﻿

4.2 Acceptable Use

Approved employee-owned mobile devices as indicated in the list above, may only be used to conduct the following actions:

  • Access and read emails

  • Access and read Slack messages

  • Perform (video) calls

  • Use mobile phone as i.e. a hotspot

﻿

Further the recommendations in FISMS-020 Acceptable Use Policy must be followed when using employee-owned mobile devices.

﻿

﻿

4.3. Authentication

  • When using employee-owned mobile devices, employees must adhere to all other FISMS policies.

  • Employee-owned mobile devices must automatically be registered in the FISMS-005A Inventory of Assets by the Information Security Office.

  • Personal devices must be secured with a PIN, passphrase, or biometric authentication based on at least 6 digits.

  • Before disposing, selling, switching or handing out a device to a third-party for servicing & maintenance, the IT Operations Team must be consulted.

﻿

﻿

4.4 Change or Termination of an active Working Relationship between an Employee and Frontify

At the end of the employment (or other contractual relationship) with Frontify, all Frontify data, information and systems must be fully removed from the employee-owned mobile device, including physical and/or cloud-based backups.

﻿

The CISO at their own discretion must describe a satisfactory method of data erasure on a case-by-case basis.

﻿

At any point in time during the employment with Frontify, an employee may choose to fully remove all Frontify information from an employee-owned mobile device using a method prescribed by the CISO (e.g., when switching phones).

﻿

The employee must ensure business continuity by having a replacement device ready.

﻿

Upon removing all Frontify information, the CISO, the Information Security Office, or the appointed designee must remove the device from the FISMS-005A Inventory of Assets.

5 Reporting Incidents

Employees must immediately inform the Information Security Office in case of the following (but not limited) to scenarios:

  • unusual behavior of an employee-owned mobile device or a company-provided device,

  • security incidents or breaches that stem from accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to company data, information and systems,

  • loss or theft of an employee-owned mobile device or a company-provided device.

6 Policy Compliance

6.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

6.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

6.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

6.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
