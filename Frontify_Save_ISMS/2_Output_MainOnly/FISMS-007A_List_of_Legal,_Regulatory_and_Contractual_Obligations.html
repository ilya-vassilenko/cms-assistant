1 List of Legal, Regulatory, Contractual, & Other Requirements

1.1 General

Frontify AG is a SaaS cloud provider headquartered in Switzerland. In the past four years, Frontify expanded its global presence and established three subsidiaries across Europe and the United States, namely in New York, USA - Frontify Inc -, in London, UK - Fronitfy UK Ltd - and in Frankfurt, Germany - Frontify Deutschland GmbH. In 2023 Frontify has acquired a SaaS Start-Up called TWICPICS SAS with registered offices in Paris France, forming the fourth subsidiary of Frontify (altogether "Frontify").

﻿

Frontify must comply first and foremost with the Swiss legal and regulatory framework in its entirety. This also applies in connection to any field of activity and interest of Frontify, such as labor law obligations, obligations of data privacy, financial duties, data retention, security measures, and contract law.

﻿

﻿

1.2 Labour Law

Due to the presence of the Frontify group with legal entities in five different countries, Frontify has the obligation to employ its workforce in compliance with all relevant legal and regulatory Swiss, US, UK, German, and French labor law requirements.

﻿

Due to the nature of industry in which Frontify operates, there might be the need to perform pre-screening checks on candidates for certain key roles. Such checks can include, but are not limited to, reference checks, migration status checks (eligibility to work legally in that country), criminal background checks, and educational checks as set forth in FISMS-019 Human Resources Security Policy. Frontify will perform such pre-screening checks in accordance with applicable local laws and, where legally required, the express consent of the candidate.

﻿

To ensure a full compliance with relevant labor laws outside of Switzerland, Frontify has engaged different professional employment organizations, which assist Frontify for duties mainly connected to payroll, social security, sickness leave, compulsory local employee trainings, and health & safety regulations.

﻿

Frontify also benefits from the services of professionals who live in countries outside of Frontify's five main hubs. To serve this purpose, Frontify has engaged an employer-of-record organization called Oyster, which ensures compliance with local labor laws through their local subsidiaries or their extended network of local umbrella employment companies operating in the countries where the professionals work.

﻿

To the extent compliant with local labor laws, all employees and external professionals engaged by Frontify, shall abide to our internal policies, including but not limited to security, confidentiality, privacy, health & safety, and corporate social responsibility. Frontify requires that they all sign the Frontify Statement of Acceptance of the relevant FISMS policies and take part in the relevant security and compliance trainings. When deemed relevant by Frontify, external professionals might also be required to sign additional non-disclosure agreements and contracts for the assignment of IP rights to Frontify.

﻿

In 2021 due to the fast development of the product and services offered, Frontify decided to benefit from the professional services of a Swiss company called Interventure Sourcing AG, which employs engineers in Serbia and that is already considered an extremely valuable player in such market. The service agreement signed between Frontify and Interventure includes high standards of confidentiality, security, IP rights, and compliance with all current privacy regulations.

﻿

﻿

2.3 Contract Law

The principles of compliance with all relevant applicable Swiss laws also apply in connection to the contractual relationship between Frontify and its customers. Frontify always favors agreeing to the relevant national and international legal principles applicable to B2B contractual obligations arising between a SaaS cloud provider and the legal entity purchasing our subscription (as enshrined in Swiss and European International Private Law). In general the principle of proximity shall apply. This shall translate in the contract being governed by the laws of the country where the party who will perform obligations characteristic of the service provided has its habitual residence or central administration. For this reason, the general terms and conditions of Frontify state that the contractual relationship between Frontify and its customers is governed by the laws of Switzerland only, without regard to any conflict of law provisions and that in case of dispute, the parties will elect the courts of St. Gallen to be competent to rule.

﻿

However, due to the nature of the service licensed by Frontify as well as Frontify's expanding global presence, it might happen that certain customers located outside Switzerland would require the contract to be governed by different applicable laws than Swiss law. In that case, the primary legal framework might change or can be complemented by another legal framework that ensures a fair balance of financial, reputational, and security risks between the customer and Frontify. Thus, as of today, Frontify’s processes have evolved to meet such global demands, and our terms and conditions are also tailored in order to ensure such fair balance in accordance with the requirements of the laws of New York, Germany, and the United Kingdom. However, any amendment to our standard law and jurisdiction clause must happen only upon request by the customer and still be evaluated on a case-to-case basis.

﻿

﻿

2.4 Privacy Law

The respect of privacy rights for all categories of individuals whose personal data are processed by Frontify is one of the main pillars of Frontify's code of conduct. Frontify must comply, first and foremost, with the applicable Swiss privacy rules and regulations. In addition, Frontify is also subject to respecting European and other foreign data privacy laws that will apply to Frontify as the processing entity as described in the Frontify Privacy Policy and the Data Protection Agreement.

﻿

Due to the nature of the business of Frontify, Frontify shall also ensure that its entire ecosystem of vendors and service providers respect relevant national and international privacy standards as described to in FISMS-015 Security Considerations & Clauses for Suppliers and Partner.

4 Updates of Legal Requirements

The Frontify Legal Team relies on various sources and mechanisms to stay updated about changes in the legal panorama (new laws, new judicial decisions) in order to be able to take the necessary actions in the time required.

﻿

﻿

4.1 Know How resources

The Legal Team holds a weekly team meeting to discuss current issues and concerns of the daily business, as well as to exchange news and updates on new or the changes in the legal requirements applicable to fields of interest of Frontify. The Legal Team gathers information about such new or changing laws and decisions from various sources:

  • Lexology Pro: a software dedicated to legal research and updates

  • Employee's know-how and professional area of expertise

  • Know-How exchange of Legal Team members

  • Know-How exchange with external professionals

  • External education (University, webinars, etc.) of individual Legal Team members

  • Websites and newsletters of recognized governmental and private interest organizations (e.g. European Data Protection Board, EU Commission, Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner)

﻿
