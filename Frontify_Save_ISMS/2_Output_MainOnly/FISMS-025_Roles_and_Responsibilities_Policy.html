2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

This document summarizes the roles and responsibilities that are relevant at Frontify. The assignment of roles to individual employees or teams is not part of this document.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

Users of this document are all employees of Frontify.

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

Security – The ISO 27001 standard does not require a company to nominate neither Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) nor Information Technology Security Officer (ITSO), or any other explicit parties who would coordinate information security (e.g., Information Security Officer, Security Manager, etc.). The standard is applicable to companies of any size, in any industry, therefore requiring small companies to always have a designated CISO would be an overkill. Nevertheless, Frontify takes security very seriously and thus appointed a CISO.

﻿

Data Privacy – Also, in the privacy field, there are different levels of requirements – e.g., many countries do not require a company to designate a Data Privacy Officer (DPO), on the other hand General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) requires appointing a DPO if the company processes personal data on a large scale or fulfills other relevant criteria. The overall trend in the privacy field goes in direction of clearly assigned roles and responsibilities. Frontify has appointed an internal DPO to coordinate all efforts on privacy.

3 Roles and Responsibilities Matrix

When assigning roles and responsibilities, for both information security and privacy functions the following overview must be taken into account.

﻿

﻿

3.1 Required Security and Privacy Roles

﻿

3.1.1 Security

Role

Responsibilities

Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
(1 FTE)

  • Coordinate all the activities related to securing the information in the company:
  • Propose, review, or revise information security objectives.
  • Identify security requirements, best practices (e.g., NIST, COBIT5) and inputs from special interest groups.
  • Establish, review, or revise an Information Security Policy and other information security policies.
  • Ensure the non-technical implementation of the policies.
  • Establish and conduct regular performance measurements of the information security framework and communicate results.
  • Coordinate and perform regular risk assessments, determine the level of risk and ensure the corresponding risk treatment. Notify the Senior Management about the main risks.
  • Pro-actively look for reducing potential risk of data incidents and data breaches.
  • Carry out the security awareness trainings and ongoing information sessions (if required).
  • Ensure that all corrective actions are performed, and the cause of non-conformities is eliminated.
  • Instruct employees on how to operate local systems in Frontify environment.
  • Define required security features of Internet services.
  • Define which type of communication channels are acceptable and which are not.
  • Dispose of local media and equipment no longer in use, in a secure way.
  • Approve appropriate methods for the protection of mobile devices, computer networks, and other communication channels.
  • Propose authentication methods, password policy, encryption methods, etc.
  • Ensure third parties are compliant with Frontify security requirements.
  • Manage security incidents (receive the information, coordinate responses, analyze incidents to prevent their future occurrence).
  • Participate in the organization’s incident response team.
  • Ensure that Frontify will be ISO-27001 certified.
  • Appoint a deputy in case of temporary unavailability (e.g., vacation, sick leave)

CISO representative
(0.5 FTE)

  • The CISO representative shall support the CISO in as many tasks as possible. Basically, the CISO representative should know about all topics to a certain degree.

Security Operations Lead

(1 FTE)

  • Oversee day-to-day operations of the Security Operations Center (SOC)
  • Develop and maintain incident response plans and playbooks
  • Ensure technical implementation of the identified security requirements.
  • Propose security improvements and corrective actions.
  • Monitor public channels regarding known security threats and execute proactive and corrective actions if applicable.
  • Instruct employees on how to operate cloud systems in Frontify environment.
  • Ensure that the relevant corrective actions are performed, and the cause of non-conformities is eliminated.
  • Collaborate with other teams (ITOps, DevOps, engineering, etc.) on security matters
  • Own continuous improvement of threat detection and response processes
  • Support security assessments, audits, and investigations
  • Participate in the organization’s incident response team.

Security Operations Specialist

(1.5 FTE)

  • Monitor public channels regarding known security threats and execute proactive and corrective actions if applicable.
  • Ensure that the relevant corrective actions are performed, and the cause of non-conformities is eliminated.
  • Monitor security alerts, logs, and events using tools like SIEM and EDR
  • Escalate critical issues according to incident response procedures
  • Assist in vulnerability management and patch tracking
  • Support forensic investigations when needed
  • Perform threat hunting and analyze potential indicators of compromise (IOCs)
  • Investigate and respond to security incidents and suspicious activity
  • Participate in the organization’s incident response team.

Director of Architecture

(1 FTE)

  • Define principles for secure development of information systems.

Security Information Response Team (SIRT)

  • Respond to information security incidents

VP Technology

  • Ensure implementation of relevant security recommendations within engineering and infrastructure teams
  • Act as risk owner for code and application-related risks
  • Enable and support the engineering team in applying secure engineering practices
  • Ensure timely resolution of identified security bugs and vulnerabilities
  • Align technical decision-making with information security objectives and risk appetite
  • Collaborate with the Information Security Office to assess the security impact of architectural or tooling changes

VP Technical Operations

  • Act as risk owner for cloud infrastructure and related operational environments
  • Provide adequate resources to support the implementation and remediation of security controls, fixes, and improvements
  • Ensure that disaster recovery processes are in place, regularly tested, and effective in supporting business continuity requirements
  • Collaborate with the Information Security Office to evaluate and mitigate operational security risks

3.1.2 Privacy and Security

Role

Responsibilities

Senior Management (Executive team designate, such as CEO or VP of People)

  • Provide sponsorship and accountability/ownership for privacy and security topics in the company (e.g., provide budget and resources for the execution of security measurements, assessments and improvements; advocacy across the company).
  • Conduct reviews of policy changes (at least annually; additional reviews if required). The purpose of reviews is to establish the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of policies.

3.1.3 Privacy

Role

Responsibilities

Data Protection Officer (DPO) & Legal Team

  • Set up, review, and ensure the implementation of an annual Compliance Implementation Plan. The plan should, at minimum, include the following working items (if applicable):
  • Establishing, reviewing, or revising privacy related policies and privacy related procedures (e.g., Privacy and Cookie Policy, Employee Privacy (Codes of Conduct).
  • Identify legal requirements (e.g., external legal and regulatory requirements, contractual requirements).
  • Monitoring or auditing the organization’s privacy-related practices.
  • Working with developers, designers, or marketing representatives to design privacy protections into new products, services, or promotions.
  • Managing the Privacy Risk Assessment processes.
  • Define security clauses that must be part of third-party agreements.
  • Ensure third parties are compliant with Frontify privacy requirements.
  • Manage security incidents (prepare legal actions).
  • Following privacy related legal developments and trends (country-specific, European Union, cross-border data transfer).
  • Holding regular cross-functional privacy meetings to discuss policy events that might impact the company, the deployment of new policies and procedures, and to provide a forum to employees from other functional groups to raise issues and receive feedback regarding new product features, business lines, marketing events, etc. The issues discussed, and the conclusions reached will be documented for future reference and broader dissemination, as appropriate.
  • Manage relationships as appropriate with DPAs in markets relevant to Frontify. This includes reporting significant privacy developments to DPAs as required by law and proactively seeking feedback on privacy protections, where required by law or otherwise seen by the company as strategic.
  • Training employees (e.g., providing core privacy training to most employees, as well as specialized privacy training for employees that have access to personal information).
  • Responding to privacy related complaints or questions.
  • Assisting the organization in negotiating contracts in which the organization is providing privacy related representations, warranties, guarantees, or indemnification (e.g., client-facing agreements).
  • Assisting the organization when negotiating privacy provisions in contracts in which the organization is providing data to third parties (e.g., reviewing privacy practices of vendors and negotiating appropriate contractual guarantees).
  • Participating in the organization’s incident response team.
  • Pro-actively looking for reducing potential risk of privacy breaches.
  • Developing and maintaining a Data Inventory and a Data Map.
  • Reporting to Senior Management any significant privacy related risks or concerns.
  • Managing the cross-border transfer of information between jurisdictions with different privacy standards.
  • Incorporating privacy and data protection best practices (Privacy by Design, OECD guidelines).
  • Respond to data subject requests (export data, opt-out, get access to the data).
  • Appoint a deputy in case of temporary unavailability (e.g., vacation, sick leave).

3.1.4 Role authorities

The CISO and CPO have the right to act at their own discretion and must always be able to report directly to senior management.

﻿

The DPO and the Legal Team may only act after consultation with the CISO.

﻿

﻿

3.2 Responsibilities of All Employees

Employees must adhere to the following obligations when it comes to information security and privacy responsibilities:

﻿

﻿

3.2.1 Security

Role

Responsibilities

All employees

  • Read and understand all applicable FISMS policies.
  • Be compliant with applicable FISMS policies.
  • Report security incidents and breaches to the CISO or to security@frontify.com.
  • Attend the regular FISMS training.
  • Advocate the importance of security towards their colleagues and/or direct reports.
  • Protect integrity, availability, and confidentiality of assets owned by the employee.

3.2.2 Privacy

Role

Responsibilities

All employees

  • Report data incidents, data breaches and privacy breaches to the DPO/Legal Team or to security@frontify.com.
  • Be aware of the contractual obligations and be compliant with them.
  • Understand Data Inventory and ensure that DPO/Legal Team is aware of any practices that might force a change to either document. If such a practice is discovered retroactively, report the practice immediately.
  • Register new data sources (e.g., new Tools) in the Software & Data Inventory.
  • Notify DPO whenever the collection, storage, disclosure, retention, and deletion policy may change or is required to be changed. Contribute to keeping the Software & Data Inventory up to date.
  • Attend the security and privacy trainings.
  • Participate in regular privacy meetings as appropriate.

3.3 Other Security and Privacy Related Roles

﻿

3.3.1 Privacy

Role

Responsibilities

Design and Marketing

  • Execute actions in consideration of the relevant laws and regulations and contract commitments.
  • Consult with DPO/Legal Team and get an approval prior to publishing privacy-relevant material.

Sales, Customer Success, Partnerships

For or any new contract and/or a new relationship:

  • Consult with DPO/Legal Team if a new contract would require an adjustment of the Privacy Policy and if it is compliant with the existing partnership contracts and obtain an approval, consult with the legal team that the new contract is compliant with applicable laws and regulations.
  • Make sure all signed versions of all NDAs, contracts and other legal documents are properly stored according to the current company policy.

Employee Success

  • Treat the employee’s and applicant’s information according to the local laws and regulations and Frontify internal guidelines.

4 Policy Compliance

4.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

4.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

4.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

4.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
