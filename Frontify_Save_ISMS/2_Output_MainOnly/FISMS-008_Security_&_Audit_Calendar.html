2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of this document is to ensure that the relevant employees know when and what security & audit tasks are to be performed.


﻿2.4 Scope

This document is applied to the entire Frontify Information Security Management System (FISMS) scope, i.e., to all types of information, regardless of the form – paper or electronic documents, applications and databases, people's knowledge, etc.


﻿2.3 Priciniple

The priciniple is that all events are tracked and traceable.

3.1.6 Penetration tests which are performed by customers

Customers have the option to perform penetration tests.

However, some requirements must be met for this:

  1. Customer must fall into the Enterprise segment

  2. The test must be announced at least 4 weeks in advance

  3. Testing may only be performed on the test infrastructure designed for this purpose

  4. The customer must agree that we will do an internal assessment of the vulnerabilities and the fix will happen based on our assessment

3.2 Bug Bounty Program

Frontify must run a private Bug Bounty program backed with an appropriate amount of money. The scope should cover at least the core application.

﻿

Up-to-date reports must be available in Bugcrowd's Reports Dashboard.

﻿

﻿

3.3 Vulnerability Scans

Vulnerability scans must be performed at least weekly on

  • Application

  • Servers

  • Clients

﻿

3.4 Risk, Threats & Vulnerability Assessments

The different risk assessments should be performed at least once a year according to the FISMS-013 Risk Management Policy.
﻿
﻿The different threat and vulnerability assessments should be performed on a case-to-case basis according to the FISMS-013 Risk Management Policy.

﻿

﻿

Firewall rules must be reviewed at least once a year to assess their effectiveness in protecting our network, systems, and data. The review must also take into consideration if we align with industry best practices and relevant regulations.

6 Compliance Measurement

6.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

6.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

6.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

6.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
