2 Introduction

2.1 Purpose

The purpose of the policy is to prevent unauthorized physical access, damage, and interference to the organization’s information and information processing facilities.

﻿

﻿

2.2 Scope

All company-owned or leased premises or locations that are deemed in scope by the scope statement. Out of scope is the third-party and supplier physical and environmental security.

﻿

All employees and third-party users.

﻿

﻿

2.3 Principle

The Physical and environmental security policy is built on the principle of exceeding Health and Safety regulations whilst protecting the most sensitive physical assets based on risk.

﻿

While updating this policy, the policy owner must keep in mind that Frontify has no infrastructure other than laptops that process data. The buildings are used as office space or hubs for meeting and exchanging.

3 Physical and Environmental Security Policy

3.1 Physical Security Perimeter

The physical perimeter of the building or site containing information processing facilities is physically sound. The exterior roof, walls, and flooring of the site are of solid construction, and all external doors are suitably protected against unauthorized access with control mechanisms like:

  • Key cards

  • Keys

Doors and windows are locked when unattended.

﻿

Access to sites and buildings is restricted to authorized personnel.

﻿

Suitable intruder detection systems are installed to national, regional, or international standards.

﻿

﻿

3.2 Secure Areas

3.2.1 Work Areas (Zone 1)

Work areas are low-security areas with some controls. They provide access controls to information and physical assets where any loss would result in a business impact that's up to very high. They also provide some protection for people.

﻿

Zone 1 areas allow unrestricted access for employees and contractors. Public or visitor access is restricted.

﻿

Examples of work areas are:

  • Frontify office environments

  • Home-based worksites where the employees can control access.

There are no restrictions in Zone 1 for employees or contractors.

﻿

﻿

3.2.2 Public Access Areas (Zone 2)

These are unsecured areas, including out-of-office working arrangements. They provide limited access controls to information and physical assets where any loss would result in a low to medium business impact. They also provide limited protection for employees. When working in Zone 2, employees always have the FISMS-022 Remote Work Policy to keep in mind.

﻿

Examples of public access areas are:

  • Coffee bar / Kitchen

  • Everything outside an official Frontify working area

Employees have permission to work on their Frontify laptops without restriction.

﻿

For meetings or other discussions in Zone 2, the employee must ensure that only authorized persons near the distance can hear or see the pieces of information.

﻿

﻿

3.3 Visitor Access

Visitors should be registered in advance via Slack with the People Support team. The People Support team shall welcome guests and escort them to the right place. If the People Support team is unavailable, the employee who opened the door automatically takes over this task.

﻿

Once the guests are in the right place, the following rules apply:

  • Guests should always be in the meeting room or public areas (e.g. Kitchen, coffee room). Working areas should be avoided for longer stays.

  • Guests shall be escorted by the designated personnel, and their activities, if required, shall be monitored.

  • Guests are only allowed to connect to the Frontify-Guest Wifi.

﻿

﻿

3.4 Delivery and Loading Areas

Frontify does not have any delivery and loading areas.

﻿

﻿

3.5 Network Access, Cabling, and Equipment Security

Unused network jacks must be deactivated by default.

﻿

Access to cable rooms must be restricted by physical access control.

﻿

Storage facilities should be secured to avoid unauthorized access.

﻿

Wifi must be separated into different wireless network segments.

4 Policy Compliance

4.1 Compliance Measurement

The Information Security Office will verify compliance to this policy through various methods, including but not limited to, business tool reports, internal and external audits, and feedback to the policy owner.

﻿

﻿

4.2 Exceptions

Any exception to the policy must be approved and recorded by the Information Security Office in advance and reported to the CISO.

﻿

﻿

4.3 Non-Compliance

An employee found to have violated the Frontify Information Security Management System may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

﻿

﻿

4.4 Continual Improvement

The policy is updated and reviewed as part of the continual improvement process.
