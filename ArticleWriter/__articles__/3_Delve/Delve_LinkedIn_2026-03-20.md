Allegations of Fabricated Compliance Reports: What Companies Should Know

A detailed investigation published on March 19, 2026 alleges that Delve, a YC-backed compliance automation platform that raised $32M at a $300M valuation, systematically produced pre-generated SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, and GDPR compliance reports for hundreds of clients.

According to the investigation by DeepDelver — written by an affected Delve client who collaborated with others — a leaked Google Spreadsheet linked to approximately 575 confidential audit reports. The analysis claims that 493 out of 494 SOC 2 reports contained identical boilerplate text, with only company names, logos, and signatures differing. The investigation further alleges that auditor conclusions were pre-written before clients provided their company descriptions, which, if true, would constitute a violation of AICPA independence rules.

Delve's CEO Karun Kaushik responded in December 2025 by characterizing the original exposing email as "AI-generated" with "falsified claims" and stated that the leak was limited to a single document made publicly accessible due to human error. Delve also published a blog post in January 2026 titled "Inside Delve's Trusted Compliance Process," asserting that reports are "triple verified" and that "no two audit reports are the same."

The allegations were not entirely new. As early as November 2025, the GRC Uncensored podcast had flagged concerns about Delve's audit practices. In January 2026, the Daily Compliance newsletter reported on compliance platforms selling pre-filled audit reports — without naming Delve directly. Independent reviews have also documented gaps in Delve's integration library and customer experience.

It is important to note that these are allegations. The investigation is authored by an affected party, and Delve has disputed the claims. No regulatory body has publicly confirmed the findings as of this date.

Regardless of how this specific case develops, it raises questions every company relying on third-party compliance automation should consider:

→ Request and review the actual audit report — not just a trust page or badge
→ Verify the independence and credentials of the auditing firm that signed the report
→ Ask your compliance vendor how evidence is collected, tested, and validated
→ Understand whether auditor conclusions are formed independently or generated by the platform
→ If your compliance certifications underpin contractual obligations or regulatory requirements (HIPAA, GDPR), confirm that the underlying process would withstand regulatory scrutiny

The broader compliance automation market is under increasing pressure to deliver speed. Gartner research cited in industry reporting estimates that 80% of AI audits are "fundamentally flawed." Speed is valuable — but not at the cost of substance.
